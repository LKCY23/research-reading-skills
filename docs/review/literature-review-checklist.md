# Literature-review review checklist

Use this checklist when reviewing outputs from the Layer 2 `literature-review` workflow.

## Scope clarity
- Is the topic boundary explicit rather than implied?
- Does the research question stay narrow enough to review?
- Do inclusion and exclusion criteria explain what is in scope and out of scope?
- Are known scope risks visible early?

## Coverage-state honesty
- Does each paper clearly declare `seed_only`, `partial_layer1`, or `full_layer1`?
- Are missing Layer 1 artifacts visible instead of hidden?
- Does the review avoid treating partial or seed-only coverage like a grounded full read?

## Comparison usefulness
- Are the comparison axes specific enough to help a reviewer compare papers?
- Do comparison cells stay tied to the declared axes?
- Does the matrix help the reader see what is comparable and what is not?

## Visible missing evidence
- Are missing evidence and coverage warnings explicit?
- Are comparison limits stated where evidence is thin or unavailable?
- Could a reviewer quickly identify which papers need deeper Layer 1 work next?

## Grounded synthesis
- Does the taxonomy reflect the actual paper set rather than generic survey prose?
- Are evidence patterns grounded in the comparison matrix and coverage notes?
- Are gaps and disagreements presented as reviewable issues rather than overconfident conclusions?
- Is the recommended reading order justified by coverage and review goals?

## Explicit review limits
- Does `review_limits` state what this review cannot yet support?
- Do the limits acknowledge seed-only dependence, missing artifacts, or thin evidence?
- Do `next_actions` point to concrete follow-up work instead of vague future improvement?
